Client

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I decided to use content and images that were familiar to me. I used some pictures I have taken for myself, I used some pictures that I have seen online before, and I even used a screenshot of the video that first taught me how to solve a Rubik's Cube.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

I mainly communicated with my developer through text. He had a lot of questions, and I was happy to help him. Since everything was mainly over text, we had a couple times when we didn't know what each other were saying, but overall, the communication went well.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

I started on my final project a bit before we talked about it in class, and I had a lot of fun things planned for myself. When we learned that other people were going to do it for us, I was a little bummed because I was looking forward to some exciting things. But overall, this website looks a lot better than I anticipated. I am glad they made it work out well.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

I think the main thing I will do differently is look at the website more often, and have more to suggest for them to fix or change, if anything.

Lead Developer

How did you manage your development team and communicate the tasks for your junior devs?

The main way I managed my development team was by messaging over our group chat to make sure that we were all doing alright. I should have done better, seeing as one of the child pages still isn't done, but overall, I just kept encouraging them and trying to help them.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?

I actually sit by my client in class, so I talked to him in class sometimes, while also texting him. There were a couple times that things didn't make sense in the site plan, but by talking to him, I was able to understand it.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?

I published my clients site very early one, and just added onto the code. As soon as I created the repository, I shared the url with my client so they could see the progress and correct us if we messed something up.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

It was a lot of fun. It was a little tricky to get used to, but once I did, I was easily to publish things and make sure that the pages were updating and showing correctly.
